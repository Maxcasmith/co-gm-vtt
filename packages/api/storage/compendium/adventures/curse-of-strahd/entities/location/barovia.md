>>Barovia is a land of ghosts, werewolves, and other fell creatures. The wilderness hides many secrets, including forgotten ruins and battlefields that tell the story of Strahd's life as a conqueror. Adventurers who explore the wilderness find the remnants of Strahd's ancient enemies, not all of them as dead as one might expect.

The cursed realm of Barovia is shrouded in mist, making it a place of despair and danger, ruled by the vampire Strahd von Zarovich. The town of Barovia is marked by oppression and hopelessness, being the most affected by Strahd's reign.
## DM Notes
Player characters may encounter various environmental hazards and remnants of Strahd’s foes as they traverse the land.

Eerie mists surround Barovia and bind its inhabitants there. The land is characterized by decay and the omnipresence of its undead lord, Strahd von Zarovich.

>>The idyllic valley nestled in the Balinok Mountains was a slice of heaven to those who knew of its existence before Strahd's arrival. The serenity of the place was forever shattered when Strahd led a bloody crusade against the enemies of his family that ended here with the slaughter of hundreds.

The serene valley of Barovia serves as Strahd's domain, now shrouded in mist and despair. This secluded area is home to both native Barovians and those who come under the influence of Strahd, creating a complex landscape of fear, subservience, and a glimmer of hope for liberation from his grasp.

Rolling thunderclouds cast a gray pall over the land of Barovia. A deathly stillness hangs over the dark woods, which are patrolled constantly by Strahd's wolves and other servitors.

The land of Barovia resides in its own demiplane, isolated from all other planes, including the Material Plane.

>> Barovia sits at the east end of the valley.
Barovia is a dreary village characterized by its somber atmosphere and the heavy influence of its dark lord, Strahd. The villagers live in fear and are wary of outsiders, creating a tense environment where trust is hard to earn.

## DM Notes
The village contains secrets that can be uncovered by players through diplomacy or investigation, introducing deeper narrative elements.

Strahd conquered this land centuries ago and named it after his father, King Barov. Strahd uses wolves, bats, and other creatures to spy on all of his realm. Barovians are simple, frightened people. Some have old souls, but many do not. The soulless ones are easy to spot, for they know nothing but fear. They have no charm, hope, or spark, and they don't cry.
